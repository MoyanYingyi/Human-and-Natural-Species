Introduction content

Life on Earth began 3.5 billion years ago, evolving from simple microbial organisms to the complex biodiversity we see today . Through natural selection, species gradually adapted to their environments, resulting in the formation of the main evolutionary branches of plants, animals, and early primates .

Modern humans originated in Africa about 300,000 years ago and share a common ancestor with older hominins such as Homo erectus . As humans moved across continents, they interacted with diverse ecosystems, impacting species through hunting, domestication, and landscape modification.
